Control and Integration of Renewable Energy





OUR ADVICE !


Ongoing concerns about climate change have made renewable energy sources an important component of the world energy consumption portfolio. Renewable energy technologies could reduce CO2 emissions by replacing fossil fuels in the power generation industry and the transportation sector.













OUR AIM !

The “grid of the future” is an imaginary, a vision that is useful to guide and energize us toward a future with less dependence on carbon based energy sources. The form and architecture of this future, networked grid is emerging and is affected by many factors: technical, social, political, and economic. As with the transition to autonomous cars this transition will take some time and during the transition competing imaginaries will emerge and be tested for viability.
